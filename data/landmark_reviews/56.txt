NAME: Koyikkal Palace
CATEGORY: Heritage Palace / Folklore & Numismatics Museum

[RAW_REVIEW_REPOSITORY]

"A quiet slice of Venad history in Feb 2026. This 17th-century two-storied Nalukettu palace at Nedumangad, built for Umayamma Rani of the Venad royal family, is beautifully preserved and rarely crowded. Feb 2026"

"WHAT'S INSIDE: Two museums under one roof. The ground-floor Numismatics Museum displays coins from Travancore, other princely states, British India and even ancient Rome. The first-floor Folklore Museum holds quaint musical instruments, occupational tools, household utensils and models of folk arts across 13 galleries. Jan 2026"

"AMBIANCE & CROWD: Calm, shaded and unhurried — a heritage stop rather than a buzzy attraction. The traditional Kerala wood architecture is the highlight even before you reach the exhibits. The on-site guides are helpful and walk you through the collections. Oct 2025"

"PRICING: A nominal entry ticket. Genuinely good value for the history on display. 2025"

"LOGISTICS: Nedumangad, about 18 km northeast of Trivandrum city. Open 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM, CLOSED ON MONDAYS. Allow around an hour. Easy to combine with a Ponmudi/Aruvikkara day since it's on that side of the city. 2024"

"LOCAL TIP: Ask a guide to point out the older Travancore and Roman coins in the numismatics gallery — it's easy to walk past how rare some of them are. Don't plan it for a Monday; that's the one day it's shut. 2024"
