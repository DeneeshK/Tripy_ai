ID: 32
NAME: Mankayam Waterfalls (Kalakayam & Kurisadi)
CATEGORY: Waterfall / Eco-Tourism / Trekking

[RAW_REVIEW_REPOSITORY]

"A refreshing forest escape in Feb 2026. The water is crystal clear and surprisingly cold. Unlike some other falls, the Forest Department has maintained the 'natural' feel here without too much concrete. The sounds of the jungle are incredible. Feb 2026"

"THE TWO FALLS: Kurisadi Falls is near the entrance and is safer for families to bathe in. Kalakayam Falls is a bit deeper into the forest and is much more powerful and dramatic. The trail between them is an easy, beautiful walk under a thick canopy. Jan 2026"

"TREKKING: For the adventurous, you can book a guided trek to 'Varayadin Motta' (the mountain of the Nilgiri Tahr). It’s a strenuous climb but offers one of the best views of the Western Ghats. You need to book these treks through the Forest office at the entrance. 2025"

"LOGISTICS & ENTRY: 
- Entry Fee: Rs 30-50 per person. Parking: Rs 20.
- Timings: 8:00 AM – 4:00 PM (Entry stops after 3:30 PM). 
- Note: The site is generally open all week, but check locally if there have been heavy rains as they close it for safety. Oct 2025"

"FACILITIES: There are basic changing rooms near Kurisadi Falls and a small children’s park. Don't expect fancy restaurants; there are only a few local tea stalls selling omelets, snacks, and bottled water near the parking area. 2025"

"WARNING: Leeches! If you are trekking during or after the rains, leeches are everywhere. Carry a small packet of salt or some tobacco to get them off. Also, the rocks in the stream are extremely slippery—be very careful when stepping. 2024"

"LOCAL TIP: Visit on a weekday if possible. On Sundays, it can get crowded with local groups, which takes away from the peaceful 'jungle' vibe. Combine this with a visit to the JNTBGRI (Tropical Botanic Garden) in Palode for a full nature day. 2024"
