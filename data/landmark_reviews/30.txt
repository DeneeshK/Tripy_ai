NAME: Keralam – Museum of History and Heritage
CATEGORY: History Museum / Interactive Heritage Site

[RAW_REVIEW_REPOSITORY]

"A brilliant walk through 3,000 years of history in Feb 2026. This isn't just a collection of dusty artifacts; the interactive touchscreens and multimedia displays make the evolution of Kerala really come alive. It's much more modern than the nearby Napier Museum. Feb 2026"

"HIGHLIGHTS: The Roman coins (Dinari) are a must-see—they prove Kerala's trade links with Rome from the time of Augustus Caesar. I also loved the life-sized replica of a 'Naalukettu' (traditional Kerala house) and the 16th-century Nataraja bronze. Jan 2026"

"LOGISTICS & PRICING: 
- Entry Fee: Rs 20 (Adults) / Rs 10 (Children). Foreigners: Rs 200.
- Still Camera: Rs 25. 
- Timings: 10:00 AM – 5:30 PM. 
- IMPORTANT: Closed on MONDAYS and Public Holidays. 2025"

"VISITOR EXPERIENCE: The museum is divided into eight thematic galleries including the Iron Age, Bronze Age, and early/medieval history. It's generally less crowded than the zoo or Napier, making it a great place for students or researchers to spend a few quiet hours. Oct 2025"

"PHOTOGRAPHY: You can pay a small fee for still photography, but video recording is strictly restricted. Using mobile phones inside for calls is also discouraged to maintain the quiet atmosphere. 2025"

"LOCAL TIP: Don't miss the 17th-century murals from Pundarikapuram Temple. The detail in the 'Vanasastha' and 'Ganapathy' paintings is incredible. Also, the building itself (Park View) is a 120-year-old architectural beauty worth admiring from the outside. 2024"
