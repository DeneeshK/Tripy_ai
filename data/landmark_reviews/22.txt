CATEGORY: Art Museum / Cultural Heritage

[RAW_REVIEW_REPOSITORY]

"An artistic pilgrimage in Feb 2026. The new Raja Ravi Varma Gallery is world-class—the lighting and climate control finally do justice to these masterpieces. Seeing 'Shakuntala' and 'Damayanti' in person is breathtaking. Feb 2026"

"COLLECTION: Incredible diversity. Beyond the Ravi Varma oils, there are works by Nicholas Roerich (Himalayan landscapes), Rabindranath Tagore, and Jamini Roy. The Tanjore miniatures and the Tibetan Thangka paintings are hidden gems that most people rush past. Jan 2025"

"LOGISTICS & PRICING: 
- Entry Fee: Rs 25 (Adults) / Rs 10 (Children). Foreigners: Rs 200.
- Timings: 10:00 AM – 4:45 PM. 
- IMPORTANT: Closed on MONDAYS and Wednesday mornings (opens after 1:00 PM on Wednesdays). 2025"

"THE EXPERIENCE: The gallery is split into multiple buildings. The main Ravi Varma gallery is a two-storey air-conditioned structure (built in 2023/24). The older Sri Chitra building houses Mughal, Rajput, and Oriental art. It’s a very quiet, meditative space compared to the noisy zoo nearby. 2025"

"PHOTOGRAPHY: Photography is strictly prohibited inside the galleries to protect the sensitive pigments. Security is quite vigilant about this. 2024"

"FACILITIES: There is a small souvenir shop where you can buy high-quality prints of Ravi Varma paintings (Rs 100-500). Wheelchairs and guides are available upon request. 2024"

"LOCAL TIP: Combine this with the Napier Museum (ID #4) as they are in the same compound. If you only have one hour, prioritize the Raja Ravi Varma building; it contains the most valuable and iconic pieces of the collection. 2024"
