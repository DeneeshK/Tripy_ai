NAME: Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple
REVIEW_COUNT: 20+ (Recent 2025-2026)

[CORE_INTELLIGENCE_SUMMARY]
A massive 18ft reclining Vishnu idol viewed through 3 doors. Highly spiritual but strictly regulated. Expect 2-4 hour wait times in general lines. VIP options are essential for time-sensitive travelers.

[PRACTICAL_LOGISTICS]
- TICKETING: 
    * General: Free (3-5 hour wait).
    * Special: ₹50 (Cuts queue significantly, ~45-60 min total).
    * VIP: ₹500 (North Entrance, ~20-30 min wait, includes prasad/flowers/photo, allows closer access to sanctum).
- DRESS_CODE: 
    * Men: Mundu/Dhoti ONLY. No shirts, no vests, no T-shirts. Angavastram (shoulder cloth) allowed.
    * Women: Saree or Set-mundu. If in pants/suits/leggings, a Dhoti MUST be wrapped over them.
    * Purchase: Available outside for ₹60 to ₹225.
- STORAGE: 
    * Phones/Bags: Cloakroom available for ₹10. 
    * Footwear: Disorganized; no proper stands mentioned, often left in shops or vehicles.

[CRITICAL_ALERTS_FOR_AUDITOR_AGENT]
- PARKING: "Big Issue." Strongly advise users to take an Auto/Taxi to the East Fort entrance.
- INFANTS/SENIORS: Highly recommend the ₹500 North Entrance ticket.
- HYDRATION: Water coolers are available inside the queue areas.
- LIGHTING: No artificial light inside. Early morning darshans (4 AM) are dimly lit by oil lamps; alert users to be highly attentive to see the idol.
- CROWD_MANAGEMENT: Staff can be pushy/rude during peak hours; warn families with toddlers.

[VIBE_MARKERS]
- "Time Travel": Feels like 200 years ago. No electronics, oil-lamp lighting, ancient granite carvings.
- "The Three Doors": 1st (Head/Linga), 2nd (Navel/Brahma), 3rd (Feet).
- "Prasad": Payasam (milk pudding) in tins for ₹100 is the top-rated souvenir.
