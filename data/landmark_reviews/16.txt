NAME: Pazhavangadi Ganapathi Temple
CATEGORY: Hindu Temple / Religious Landmark

[RAW_REVIEW_REPOSITORY]

"A powerful spiritual experience in Feb 2026. This temple is unique because of its black stone carvings and the 32 different forms of Lord Ganesha depicted on the walls. It is right on the main road, making it very accessible but also very busy. Feb 2026"

"THE COCONUT RITUAL: The main attraction for many is the 'Coconut Breaking' offering. You buy a coconut outside, and there is a designated area to smash it as a prayer for removing obstacles. It’s a rhythmic, loud, and fascinating sight. Jan 2025"

"DRESS CODE: Very strict traditional attire is required for men (Mundu/Dhoti, bare chest) and women (Saree or long skirt/skirt and blouse). They are firm about this at the entrance. Note that non-Hindus are generally not permitted inside the inner sanctum. 2025"

"VISITING HOURS: 
- Morning: 4:30 AM – 10:45 AM
- Evening: 5:00 PM – 8:30 PM
The evening 'Deeparadhana' (lamp lighting ceremony) is incredibly beautiful but very crowded. Oct 2024"

"ATMOSPHERE: Despite being on a noisy street, the interior of the temple maintains a very calm and serene vibration. The main idol of Ganesha is unique as he is depicted in a seated posture with his right leg folded. 2024"

"LOCATION & PARKING: Located just a few hundred meters from the Padmanabhaswamy Temple and the Central Railway Station. Parking is nearly impossible on the main road; it’s best to take a rickshaw or park at the nearby public lots in East Fort. 2024"

"LOCAL TIP: The temple is maintained by the Indian Army (Madras Regiment), which explains why it is exceptionally clean and well-organized compared to many other city temples. 2024"

"CAUTION: Mobile phones and cameras are strictly prohibited inside the temple premises. You will need to leave your footwear at the designated counter outside. 2024"
