NAME: CVN Kalari Sangham
CATEGORY: Cultural Centre / Kalaripayattu Martial Arts

[RAW_REVIEW_REPOSITORY]

"Watching Kerala's ancient martial art up close in Feb 2026. CVN Kalari at East Fort is one of the most respected Kalaripayattu schools in the south, founded in 1956, and you can watch the training from a small visitors' gallery. Feb 2026"

"THE EXPERIENCE: The morning sessions are the real thing — students drilling kicks, leaps, and weapon forms (sticks, swords, the flexible urumi) in the sunken earthen kalari pit. There's a visitors' gallery for about 25 people; daily practice can be watched and special demonstrations arranged with advance notice. Jan 2026"

"TIMINGS: Training runs roughly 6:00 AM to 8:30 AM in the morning and again around 5:00 PM to 7:00 PM in the evening. The early-morning slot is the most atmospheric. Allow 30-45 minutes. Oct 2025"

"BEYOND TRAINING: CVN is also known for its traditional kalari chikitsa (marma/Ayurvedic bodywork). It's a living institution, not a staged tourist show — go with that mindset. 2025"

"LOGISTICS: East Fort, Fort PO, Thiruvananthapuram — a few minutes' walk from the central bus station and very close to the Padmanabhaswamy temple. Best to call ahead (the kalari has a published number) to confirm the day's session and whether visitors can watch. 2024"

"LOCAL TIP: Come for the 6 AM session and pair it with an early Padmanabhaswamy temple darshan and a dosa breakfast nearby — they're all within the same East Fort pocket. Photography rules vary by day, so ask first. 2024"
