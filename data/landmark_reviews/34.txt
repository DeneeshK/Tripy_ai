"The ultimate Sadhya experience in Feb 2026. If you want a traditional wedding-style feast on a banana leaf any day of the year, this is the gold standard. The consistency of their flavors over the years is remarkable. Feb 2026"

"THE SADHYA: Their 'Bolgatty Sadhya' is the star attraction. It features over 20+ items including various pradhams (payasams), avial, thoran, and their signature pineapple pulissery. The service is incredibly efficient; they ladle out items with rhythmic precision. Jan 2026"

"BREAKFAST & SNACKS: While famous for lunch, their breakfast is equally good. The 'Puttu and Kadala curry' and their crispy Vadas are highly recommended by locals. It’s a great spot for a heavy, traditional start to the day. 2025"

"AMBIANCE & CROWD: It is a busy, functional dining hall. Don't expect a quiet, candlelit atmosphere. It’s loud, fast-paced, and usually packed. During lunch hours (12:30 PM – 2:30 PM), be prepared to wait for at least 15-30 minutes for a table. Oct 2025"

"PRICING: Very affordable considering the unlimited nature of the Sadhya. A full feast usually costs between Rs 250-350, offering incredible value for the variety and quality provided. 2025"

"LOGISTICS: Located at Bakery Junction, near the Russian Cultural Center. It's very central but parking is a major headache. They have a tiny parking area that is almost always full; taking an auto is much wiser. 2024"

"LOCAL TIP: They serve three different types of Payasam (dessert) with the Sadhya. Don't fill up too much on the rice; save room for the sweet finish! Also, they are very strict about hygiene and leaf-service etiquette. 2024"
