ID: 11
NAME: Lighthouse Beach (Kovalam)
CATEGORY: Beach / Pedestrian Promenade / Culinary Hub

[RAW_REVIEW_REPOSITORY]

"A quiet haven in Dec 2025. The narrow streets are strictly pedestrian-only—no cars or motorcycles allowed. The promenade is lined with cafes serving everything from authentic Kerala seafood to European dishes (Swiss, Italian, and English breakfasts). 2025"

"The 'European' vibe of Kerala. It feels like a laid-back resort town. You can hire a beach chair and umbrella for approx. Rs 100-200 per hour. The water is sparkling, though the sand has a unique black mineral tint. Jan 2025"

"CLIMBING THE LIGHTHOUSE: Entry is roughly Rs 20-50. You must leave shoes at the bottom. The view from the top is considered the best vantage point in South Kerala. There is a lift available, but it gets very crowded during sunset hours. 2024/2025"

"DINING HIGHLIGHTS: 
- Beatles Cafe: Famous Continental menu and great view of the lighthouse. 
- Swiss Cafe: Recommended for Swiss-style pizza and fish pie. 
- Mugs for Beer: Due to local regulations, many cafes serve 'special tea' (beer) in large ceramic mugs to maintain a low profile. Feb 2025"

"SWIMMING SAFETY: Lifeguards are very active and attentive. During monsoon (June-Sept), swimming is strictly prohibited due to rough swells. Even in calm seasons, they may blow whistles if you go past the 'white froth' line. 2024"

"CRITICAL NOTE: Long-time visitors (15+ years) complain about over-development and 'concrete' replacing the old palm-roofed shacks. However, for first-timers, it remains a clean and convenient 'rest and recovery' spot. Feb 2025"

"SHOPPING: The pedestrian alleys behind the beach are a maze of craft shops, tailors, and spice vendors (Assam Tea House is a local favorite). Haggling is expected. 2024"

"ACCOMMODATION WARNING: A few reports of 'conman' behavior at specific budget resorts (e.g., Orion Beach Resort). Stick to well-reviewed stays or heritage properties like Rockholm for safety. 2020/Updated 2025"
