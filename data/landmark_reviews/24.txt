ID: 24
NAME: Zam Zam Restaurant (Palayam)
CATEGORY: Middle Eastern & Malabar Restaurant / Culinary Landmark

[RAW_REVIEW_REPOSITORY]

"A Trivandrum institution in Feb 2026. You haven't truly experienced the city's food scene until you've had a Shawai at the original Palayam branch. It’s always buzzing, and the aroma of grilled chicken hitting you from the street is iconic. Feb 2026"

"SIGNATURE DISHES: The Shawaya (grilled chicken) and Al-Faham (charcoal-grilled) are the undisputed kings here. The 'Honey Al-Faham' with Butter Naan is a recent favorite (2025/26) that perfectly balances spice and sweetness. Their Chicken Fry made in 100% pure coconut oil is a throwback to their 1979 roots. Jan 2026"

"THE 'ICE FRUITS' TRADITION: Don't leave without trying their signature Ice Milk or the 'Zam Zam Special Fruit Salad.' It’s the classic way to cool down after a spicy Arabian meal. The Choconut and Mango Sandwich ice milk are local legends. Dec 2025"

"SERVICE & AMBIANCE: It is a high-volume, casual dining spot. Service is incredibly fast ('lightning speed'), but it can feel 'rushed' during peak hours (7:30 PM - 9:30 PM). The ambiance is lively and slightly noisy—better for a fun group dinner than a quiet romantic date. Oct 2025"

"PRICING & VALUE: Very reasonably priced. You can have a hearty meal for two for under Rs 800-1000. The portion sizes, especially for the Mandi and Biryani, are generous. 2025"

"LOGISTICS: Located opposite the MLA Hostel in Palayam. Parking is notoriously difficult on the main road; there is a small dedicated parking area, but it's often full. Best to take a rickshaw. Open 11:00 AM to 11:00 PM (later on Fridays). 2024"

"LOCAL TIP: The Palayam branch is the 'Original.' While there are other branches (YMR, Kazhakoottam), the vibe and consistency at Palayam remain the benchmark. Also, try their Mango Fish Curry if you want a break from the grilled meats—it’s a sleeper hit! 2024"
