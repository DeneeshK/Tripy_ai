"Informative and fun for children. A must-visit to understand concepts in Physics and Astronomy. The Planetarium show is the highlight—book online in advance as school groups often fill the slots. Mar 2024"

"The dome theater is spectacular. It features one of the biggest projection screens in South India. The visuals are crisp and the seating is comfortable, resembling a high-end multiplex. The show on the origin of communication and space exploration is particularly engaging. Oct 2022"

"SHOW LANGUAGE CRITICAL: There is only ONE English show per day, typically at 12:00 Noon. The other three slots (10:30 AM, 3:00 PM, 5:00 PM) are in Malayalam. The show will only start if there are at least 15 people in attendance. Aug 2022"

"FACILITIES & UPKEEP: While the Planetarium is 'Scientific Marvel,' the surrounding Museum and Science Park are in a 'sorry state.' Many interactive displays in the lower levels are broken, dusty, or have non-functional audio switches. The ISRO rocket display floor is better maintained. July 2019/Updated 2024"

"THEATER WARNING: The 3D and 6D theater experiences are widely criticized as 'pathetic' and 'overpriced.' The screen resolution is poor, and the 3D glasses are often scratched or of low quality. Additionally, seats in the 3D theater are cramped; tall people (165cm+) may find them uncomfortable. Mar 2022"

"LOGISTICS: Closed on Mondays. Located at PMG Junction. Ample parking is available inside the property, which is a significant plus in this busy area. Mar 2022"

"EXTRAS: Includes a Musical Fountain and Laserium (usually at 7:00 PM), a Ham Radio concept display, and a Science Park. The entry fee is very reasonable, making it a budget-friendly half-day trip for families. 2018/Updated 2024"

"LOCAL ADVICE: Plan for at least 3-5 hours if you want to explore the Maths, Robotics, and Electrical galleries along with the Planetarium show. The staff (including security and police) are generally noted as being helpful and well-behaved. May 2018"
