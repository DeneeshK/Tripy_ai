NAME: Peppara Wildlife Sanctuary
CATEGORY: Wildlife Sanctuary / Forest & Reservoir

[RAW_REVIEW_REPOSITORY]

"A proper forest day-trip from the city in Feb 2026. Peppara spreads along the catchment of the Peppara Dam on the Karamana river, on the way up to Ponmudi, and is a green, quiet break from beaches and temples. Feb 2026"

"WILDLIFE: The sanctuary protects a serious range of biodiversity — roughly 43 mammal species (elephants, sambar, lion-tailed macaque among them), 230+ bird species, plus reptiles, amphibians and fish. It's birding-rich, though big-mammal sightings are luck of the draw. Jan 2026"

"WHAT TO DO: Guided one- and two-day trekking programmes are organised through the Eco-Development Committees, and the reservoir views are lovely. This is low-key eco-tourism, not a safari park with guaranteed animal sightings. Oct 2025"

"TIMINGS & ENTRY: Open all year, roughly 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Nominal entry fee (about Rs 50 for Indians, more for foreigners). For treks, arrange ahead through the forest/EDC channels rather than just turning up. 2025"

"LOGISTICS: About 40 km from Trivandrum (and ~32 km from Ponmudi), reached via Vithura on the Trivandrum-Ponmudi road. Reviewers warn the access road is rough in patches and there are no restroom facilities — come prepared. 2024"

"LOCAL TIP: Best combined with a Ponmudi or Kallar trip since they share the same route. Carry water and snacks, start early, and treat it as a half-to-full day given the drive. 2024"
