"A nostalgia trip for locals and a treat for kids in Feb 2026. The Priyadarsini Planetarium remains the highlight—the shows are informative and the dome projection quality has been upgraded recently. It's one of the best ways to spend an afternoon learning about space. Feb 2026"

"INTERACTIVE EXHIBITS: The science galleries are a bit old-school but very functional. My kids loved the robotics section and the centrifugal force exhibits. It’s great to see a museum where you are actually encouraged to touch and move the displays. Jan 2026"

"LOGISTICS & TIMINGS: 
- Entry Fee: Separate tickets for the Museum (approx. Rs 20) and Planetarium shows (approx. Rs 60). 
- Timings: 10:00 AM – 5:00 PM. 
- IMPORTANT: The museum is CLOSED ON MONDAYS. Planetarium shows are usually at 11:00 AM, 12:00 PM, 3:00 PM, and 4:00 PM. 2025"

"FACILITIES: There is a small canteen on the premises and plenty of shaded areas in the science park. The Innovation Hub is a newer addition that focus on modern tech and DIY science projects. Oct 2025"

"VISITOR TIP: Book your planetarium tickets as soon as you arrive, as they often sell out for the afternoon shows, especially on weekends. The 3D theater is also worth the small extra fee. 2025"

"LOCATION: Right in the heart of the city near PMG Junction. It’s walking distance from the Museum/Zoo compound, making it easy to combine multiple sights in one day. Parking is available inside the gate but can get tight. 2024"
